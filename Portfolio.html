After the extraction, I needed a way to display the information in a way that was sharable and easy to edit when a drive needed to be swapped out. Due to my teams familiarity with Microsoft Access and the team of distribution I opted to develop a new database to store the nameplate information. After the first iteration of the database, I decided to further utilize the platforms relational database tools and incorporate spare parts information and schematic diagrams from other platforms within the company. PLC Signal Trap: "Its A Trap!"

  • PLC Programming
  • Variable Frequency Drive (VFD) Troubleshooting
  • Electromechanical Relay Wiring

During my time working in the plant, we had multiple incidents where a VFD would trip one or more of their temperature switches. This would cause the drive to stop working until it cooled off and the temperature switch closed again. Due to the short duration of the outage and travel time to reach the assets, we were unable to accurately determine which of the four temperature switches were tripping. As a solution to our uncertaintly, I work alongside one of our Master Electricians to develop a simple asset monitoring device that 'trapped' the signal in a PLC logic holding circuit. To implement the device, we made physical connections between the temperature sensors in the drive and their associated relays on our device. When any of the 4 temperature switches tripped, the signal would be recieved on the input of the PLC which would hold an LED indicator output until an operator was able to reach the drive. The implementation of this device has increased the efficency of our troubleshooting, reduced repair costs, and decreased VFD downtime in the plant.

Variable Frequency Drive Fault Testing

  • VFD Fault Testing
  • VFD Programming
  • Electrical Test Equipment
  • Reading and Interpreting Electrical Schematics

Due to the extreme weather conditions and the aging of our facility, we were occasionally susceptible to power dropouts which caused our drives to trip, requiring manual reset prior to returning to operation. To properly adjust the fault parameters, we designed fault tests on an isolated drive using a Omicron CMC 356 Universal Relay Test and Commisioning Tool. The basic test setup is as follows:

  1. Isolate the 4160V power supply and verify mechanical isolation between the motor output shaft and the pump.
  2. Disconnect power connections on the supply side of the 120VAC-24VDC converter and connect the Omicron 120V power supply. This 'tricks' the main control board of the drive into thinking it is powered on and able to operate normally while mitigating the high voltage risk.
  3. Run an inverted ramp signal on the Omicron 120V power supply to simulate a voltage drop below 75% expected.

This test enabled greater optimization of the fault relay programming parameters which now account for various types of common outages. As a result, we are experiencing reduced operational lost time in the event of transient power irregularities.

By the end, we acheived over 75 percent equipment connectivity at our worst perfoming site with two sites nearing perfect connectivity.
